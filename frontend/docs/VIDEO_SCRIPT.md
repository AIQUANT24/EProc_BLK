### SCENE 3: How It Works (1:15 – 2:20)

**[VISUAL: Animated flow diagram showing the NMICOV pipeline]**

**NARRATOR:**
> Here's how NMICOV works:

**[Step 1: BOM Declaration]**
> A supplier registers their product and declares every component — its origin, cost, and supplier. The system supports multi-tier Bills of Materials with nested sub-assemblies.

**[Step 2: DVA Calculation]**
> NMICOV's DVA Engine automatically computes the Domestic Value Addition percentage:
> **DVA = (Domestic Component Cost ÷ Total Product Cost) × 100**

**[Step 3: Classification]**
> Products are classified as **Class I** (≥50% DVA), **Class II** (≥20%), or **Non-Local** (<20%) — matching PPP-MII thresholds exactly.

**[Step 4: Fraud Detection]**
> Six intelligent fraud detection rules continuously scan for anomalies — suspicious DVA jumps, post-bid BOM changes, import mismatches, cost anomalies, circular supply chains, and missing data.

**[Step 5: Blockchain Anchoring]**
> Every declaration, calculation, and verification is cryptographically hashed and anchored to a permissioned Hyperledger Fabric blockchain — creating an immutable, multi-agency audit trail accessible to DPIIT, GeM, CAG, and sector ministries.

**[Step 6: Verification API]**
> External procurement platforms like GeM can verify any supplier's compliance status in real-time through NMICOV's Verification API — returning DVA score, classification, confidence level, risk assessment, and blockchain proof.
